The word "pear" has been encountered in Russian written sources since the 12th century in the form khrusha - beginning with the Studite Charter of 1193 (according to Sreznevsky); in the form grusha it has been encountered since the 14th century - beginning with the translation of Chronicles (1 Chronicles 14:14-15 - according to Sreznevsky). In the 17th century, the word "dulya" appeared as a synonym for the word "pear", borrowed, according to the dictionary of N. M. Shansky, from the Polish language (pol. dula), according to the dictionary of Ozhegov this word is regional and is applied to several varieties of small pears.
